Overview

I led the product design for the internal dashboard of Neon, a tool for predicting the most appealing thumbnails to the human eye based on a Ph.D research thesis on machine-learning at Carnegie Mellon University.

Defining the goal

The Neon team came to thoughtbot with the need to design the interface and user experience for uploading and selecting Neon-generated thumbnails for their videos. I led a week-long design sprint with the stakeholders, addressing the customers' needs and identifying how Neon can fulfill them.

Moving into a gentrification project

I guess I wasn't the only one who wanted to live in San Francisco. What began as an innocuous desire to move to the city of my dreams emerged a rocky relationship between myself and the city, where I questioned my position as a "tech worker" amidst a housing crisis and an ever-growing inequality gap amongst residents of the Bay Area.

In wanting to belong to the city in a more participatory nature beyond simply paying rent, I wanted to be in the public, deliberately blending into the street as I drew it.
