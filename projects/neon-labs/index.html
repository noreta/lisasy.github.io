Overview

In five weeks, I led the product, UX, and UI design and front-end development for the internal dashboard of Neon, a tool for predicting the most appealing thumbnails to the human eye based on a Ph.D research thesis on machine-learning at Carnegie Mellon University.

I designed and implemented their MVP that helped raise $4.1m in funding.

Project Background

The Neon team came to thoughtbot to design the interface and user experience for uploading and selecting Neon-generated thumbnails for their videos. Their budget from funding afforded them about five weeks of design and development time with thoughtbot.

As the lead product designer on this project, it was imperative for me to grasp Neon's product, business, technology, and customer. I led a week-long design sprint with the stakeholders, addressing the customers' needs and identifying how Neon can fulfill them.

While Neon came in with wireframes and mockups, we did not stay too fixated on those existing sources. We had to learn more about the product and what the customer needed before making decisions on how the application should function.

Value Proposition

Neon enables you to find the best thumbnail in your video to increase viewership.

The Customer

With the SaaS business model, we defined our primary customers for MVP to be media publishers.

High-level job stories

Now that we have a value proposition defined, how do we create a great user experience for those using this service?

With a lot of post-it notes, sketching, and whiteboarding, we identified five (5) core situations, positioning them as job stories.

Figuring out flow and information architecture

After defining our job stories, we were able to better understand our users' anticipated experiences in using Neon. Synthesizing these job stories into a holistic product and experience allowed us to preliminarily define the information architecture of Neon.

Wireframing

With all of the knowledge we have at this point, I created low-fidelity wireframes by sketching the main screens.

Front-end Development

Working in parallel with a Rails developer on this project, I began the front-end development. I wrote in HAML, SCSS, and Coffeescript. The code is not publicly available on Github.

Exploring different visual design and branding

In the browser using code, I got to explore different visual and communication design for Neon's dashboard. As a team, we liked the visual branding of the rightmost image below.

The left sidebar contained all navigational elements. While analytics was not yet built out, we still included a link to it to indicate to users that it would eventually be available in the application, fulfilling job story #4.

Shipping

We shipped the MVP in about five weeks. In this MVP, we addressed the five core job stories, included a helpful support and FAQ section, and synced the application successfully with the existing machine-learning technology in the back-end.

Funding success

In July 2014, I was delighted to learn that Neon raised $4.1 million in Series A funding.
