Overview

At Wesleyan University, I co-founded and managed a student-run design consultancy called TypeClub. As a team of eight, we worked pro bono with student organizations, official campus administrators, and other resources to provide design strategy and assist in advertising and publicity efforts.

Humble beginnings

When I first learned how to do graphic design in 2011, I discovered the absence of a design community at Wesleyan. I relied on many online resources to improve my skills as a designer, but I realized that if I wanted to make my skills worthwhile to have, I needed to apply them to real projects. After making flyers and websites for school organizations I was apart of, I wanted to find people who shared my interest in design so that we can help each other improve and use our design skills towards benefiting our local community.

Defining a process

After months of iterating on the best way to organize the team and workflow, we decided that TypeClub would be organized like this:

  • Keep a small team of all designers and one project manager.
  • Meet weekly in person.
  • Projects would be assigned to 1-3 designers at a time, keeping everyone involved.
  • Be led as a cooperative with everyone having an input in the direction of the organization.

Lessons from leadership

One of the biggest lessons I gained from this leadership experience was the importance of communicating effectively with all of my teammates. This meant not only having frequent discussion on goals and how to achieve them, but also listening to everyone's input with undivided attention.
